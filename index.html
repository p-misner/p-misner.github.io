Fulbright-Nehru Fellowship

I've recently been awarded a Fulbright Fellowship, an international research and exchange fellowship funded by the US Department of State, to study virtual reality and road safety in India. Due to Covid-19, the program has been delayed but is set to begin around July 2021.

Despite having only two percent of the world's vehicles, over ten percent of global traffic fatalaties occur in India. Of these, twenty-six percent of road crash fatalities involve Heavy Commercial Vehicles. Even among drivers aware of road laws, adherence to the laws are limited with drivers viewing them more as suggestions than strict rules. While educating drivers on current road safety laws is essential, another critical component of improving driver behavior is changing perceptions on the importance of following laws.

Over the one-year fellowship, I plan on exploring the effectiveness of using interactive virtual reality driving simulations to increase driver’s awareness of basic road laws and further, improve driver’s desire to follow the laws. I will be working with the SaveLIFE Foundation’s Anticipatory Driving and Accident Prevention Training Program and Weave Lab at IIIT-Delhi to develop a series of virtual reality simulations and data visualizations of common hazardous driving situations encountered by commercial drivers.
